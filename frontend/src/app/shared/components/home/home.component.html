Introduction

Welcome to the Alcalá Record Books. The Alcalá Record Books are a digitisation of an 18th century book of accounts from the Royal Irish College in Alcalá de Henares, a university town located to the east of the Spanish capital, Madrid. Established in 1649, the college served as training for Catholic clergy from Ireland, forming part of a network of Irish colleges on the continent. They came into existence for a complex of reasons but mainly because legal bars and state policy in Ireland deprived seventeenth and eighteenth century Irish Catholics of access to domestic secondary and university education. Alcalá college survived until 1785 when, after a series of controversies, it was absorbed into the Royal Irish College at Salamanca, Spain. The College at Salamanca remained active until 1950. Shortly after its closure, its archives, which included the Alcalá account books, were deposited in the library of St Patrick’s College, Maynooth. Today they are housed in the Russell Library, St Patrick’s College and curated in cooperation with Maynooth University Library.

The records here specifically deal with the monetary accounts as recorded by the rector of the college from 1774 to 1781 (excluding 1780 which is missing from the original manuscript).

The Digital Research Environment

This site is part of PhD thesis presented by Shane A. McGarry and the Maynooth University Department of Computer Science and has been funded in part through a grant from the Irish Research Council. The goal of this project is to create a Virtual Research Environment which allows the user construct queries through multiple modalities of engagement with the source material. This is primarily accomplished in 3 ways: the Keyword Search, the Visualisation Search, and the Interactive Dashboard (see below).

search Keyword Search

The keyword search provides the user with a standard search interface. Much like a Google search, the user can enter keywords and see a list of results. The keyword search also has an advanced search function that allows the user to specify additional criteria such as a specific year or to only include entries which contain the top n words in the corpus.

insert_chart_outlined Visualisation Search

The visualisation search provides the user with an alternative method of search engagement through the use of data visualisations. Here, the user can specify search criteria that is available in the standard keyword search, but rather than returning a list of results, the search is presented in the form of various types of charts. These charts can be manipulated, filtered, and clicked through to see the details of the data, allowing the user to eventually drill down to the individual manuscript page.

Go to Visualisation Search

dashboard Interactive Dashboard

The final component is that of the interactive dashboard which allows the user to curate research notes made on various objects across the website, as well as build out a "story" that can help explain a researcher's particular research question. This component allows for a type of constructed knowledge through the use of boundary objects that is an important factor in exploring these types of digitised manuscripts.
